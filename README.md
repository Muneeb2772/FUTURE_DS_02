# Customer Churn & Retention Analysis (SaaS Case Study)

## 📌 Problem Statement

Customer churn is one of the most critical challenges for subscription-based businesses. Losing customers directly impacts revenue, growth, and long-term sustainability.

This project analyzes customer data to:
- Identify churn patterns  
- Understand retention drivers  
- Analyze customer lifetime behavior  
- Recommend actionable strategies to reduce churn  

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies

- Python (Pandas, Seaborn) → Data cleaning and analysis  
- Power BI → Dashboard creation and business storytelling  

---

## 📂 Dataset Overview

The dataset contains customer-level information, including:
- Demographics (gender, senior citizen status)  
- Subscription details (contract type, services)  
- Financial data (monthly charges)  
- Customer tenure  
- Churn status  

---

## 📊 Dashboard Overview

The Power BI dashboard is divided into 4 key sections:

---

## 🔹 Page 1 — Executive Overview

  ![Executive Overview](https://github.com/Muneeb2772/FUTURE_DS_02/blob/main/Executive%20Overview.png)

This page provides a high-level summary of the business.

### Key Metrics:
- Total Customers  
- Churn Rate  
- Average Tenure  
- Average Monthly Charges  

### Key Insights:
- A significant portion of churn is concentrated among short-term (month-to-month) customers  
- The overall churn rate highlights a major retention challenge  

---

## 🔹 Page 2 — Customer Segmentation (Who is Churning?)

![Customer Segmentation](https://github.com/Muneeb2772/FUTURE_DS_02/blob/main/Customer%20Segmentation%20Analysis.png)

This page identifies which customer groups are most likely to churn.

### Key Insights:
- Customers on month-to-month contracts have the highest churn rate  
- Customers using electronic payment methods show higher churn  
- Lack of Tech Support and Online Security strongly increases churn  

### Business Interpretation:
Certain customer segments are significantly more at risk, allowing for targeted retention strategies.

---

## 🔹 Page 3 — Retention & Customer Lifetime Analysis

![Retention Analysis](https://github.com/Muneeb2772/FUTURE_DS_02/blob/main/Retention%20%26%20Customer%20Lifetime.png)

This page focuses on when customers churn and how long they stay.

### Key Insights:
- Most churn occurs within the first 12 months  
- Long-term customers are significantly more stable  
- Early-stage customer experience is critical  

### Business Interpretation:
Improving onboarding and early engagement can significantly improve retention.

---

## 🔹 Page 4 — Revenue & Business Impact

![Revenue Impact](https://github.com/Muneeb2772/FUTURE_DS_02/blob/main/Revenue%20Risk%20Analysis.png)

This page evaluates the financial impact of churn.

### Key Insights:
- A large portion of revenue is lost from early-stage customers  
- Month-to-month customers contribute the most to revenue loss  
- High-paying customers leaving early create significant business risk  

### Business Interpretation:
Reducing churn among high-value and new customers can protect revenue and improve profitability.

---

## 📈 Key Insights Summary

- Short-term contracts are the primary driver of churn  
- Early customer lifecycle (first 12 months) is the most critical period  
- Support services (Tech Support, Online Security) reduce churn significantly  
- Higher monthly charges are associated with increased churn risk  

---

## 💡 Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following actions are recommended:

- Introduce incentives for long-term contracts  
- Improve onboarding experience for new customers  
- Bundle Tech Support and Security services into core plans  
- Identify high-risk customers early and target them with retention campaigns  
- Offer loyalty rewards for long-term customers  

---

## 📊 Business Impact Summary

The analysis shows that churn is primarily driven by short-term contracts and early-stage disengagement.

By focusing on onboarding, long-term commitment strategies, and service improvements, the business can reduce churn and protect recurring revenue.
